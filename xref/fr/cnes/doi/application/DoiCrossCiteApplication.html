32  /**
33   * Provides an application to get citation from a registered DOI. Books and journal articles have
34   * long benefited from an infrastructure that makes them easy to cite, a key element in the process
35   * of research and academic discourse. In this mind, a data should cited in just the same way.
36   * DataCite DOIs help further research and assures reliable, predictable, and unambiguous access to
37   * research data in order to:
38   * <ul>
39   * <li>support proper attribution and credit</li>
40   * <li>support collaboration and reuse of data</li>
41   * <li>enable reproducibility of findings</li>
42   * <li>foster faster and more efficient research progress, and</li>
43   * <li>provide the means to share data with future researchers</li>
44   * </ul>
45   * DataCite also looks to community practices that provide data citation guidance. The Joint
46   * Declaration of Data Citation Principles is a set of guiding principles for data within scholarly
47   * literature, another dataset, or any other research object (Data Citation Synthesis Group 2014).
48   * <p>
49   * The FAIR Guiding Principles provide a guideline for the those that want to enhance reuse of their
50   * data (Wilkinson 2016).
51   * <p>
52   * <b>Security</b><br>
53   * --------------<br>
54   * No authentication / no autorisation
55   * <p>
56   * <b>Routing</b><br>
57   * --------------<br>
58   * <br>
59   * root<br>
60   * |<br>
61   * |__ style<br>
62   * |__ language<br>
63   * |__ format<br>
64   *
65   * @see <a href="http://citation.crosscite.org/">Making a citation</a>
66   * @author Jean-Christophe Malapert (jean-christophe.malapert@cnes.fr)
67   */

138     /**
139      * Creates router the DOICrossCiteApplication. This routes routes the following resources:
140      * <ul>
141      * <li>{@link DoiCrossCiteApplication#STYLES_URI} to access to the different styles for a
142      * citation</li>
143      * <li>{@link DoiCrossCiteApplication#LANGUAGE_URI} to access to the different languages for a
144      * citation</li>
145      * <li>{@link DoiCrossCiteApplication#FORMAT_URI} to access to the formatted citation</li>
146      * </ul>
147      *
148      * @return router
149      */
150     @Override
151     public Restlet createInboundRoot() {
152         LOG.traceEntry();
153 
154         final Router router = new Router(getContext());
155         router.attach(STYLES_URI, StyleCitationResource.class);
156         router.attach(LANGUAGE_URI, LanguageCitationResource.class);
157         router.attach(FORMAT_URI, FormatCitationResource.class);
158 
159         return LOG.traceExit(router);
160     }
